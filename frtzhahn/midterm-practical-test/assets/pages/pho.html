Phở

Traditional Vietnamese beef noodle soup with aromatic broth

Prep: 30 min Cook: 4-6 hours Servings: 6-8

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs beef bones
  • 2 lbs beef brisket
  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 large onions, halved
  • 4 inch piece ginger, halved lengthwise
  • 5 star anise
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 6 cloves
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles (bánh phở)
  • Fresh herbs (Thai basil, cilantro, mint)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Jalapeño or chili slices
  • Hoisin sauce and sriracha for serving

Instructions

  1. Boil beef bones for 5 minutes, drain and rinse to remove impurities
  2. Char onions and ginger over open flame until blackened
  3. Toast star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and coriander in dry pan until fragrant
  4. Place bones in large pot with 6 quarts water, bring to boil
  5. Add brisket, charred onions and ginger, toasted spices
  6. Reduce heat and simmer for 3-4 hours, skimming foam regularly
  7. Remove brisket after 2 hours, let cool and slice thinly
  8. Strain broth through fine mesh, discard solids
  9. Season broth with fish sauce and sugar to taste
  10. Cook rice noodles according to package directions
  11. Divide noodles into bowls, top with sliced brisket and raw beef
  12. Pour hot broth over to cook raw beef
  13. Serve with herbs, bean sprouts, lime, jalapeño, and sauces

Cooking Tips

Longer simmering makes richer broth, 6-8 hours is ideal. Parboiling bones removes impurities for clear broth. Charring onions and ginger adds depth of flavor. Keep broth at gentle simmer, not rolling boil. Slice beef paper thin and partially freeze for easier slicing. Fresh rice noodles are best, but dried work too.
