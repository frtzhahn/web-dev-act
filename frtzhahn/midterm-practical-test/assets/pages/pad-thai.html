Pad Thai

Classic Thai stir fried noodles with tamarind sauce

Prep: 20 min Cook: 15 min Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dried rice noodles (pad thai noodles)
  • 8 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Dried chili flakes
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water for 30 minutes until soft, drain
  2. Mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar for sauce
  3. Heat wok or large pan over high heat
  4. Add oil and fry garlic and shallots until fragrant
  5. Add shrimp and cook until pink
  6. Push shrimp to side, add beaten eggs to center
  7. Scramble eggs then mix with shrimp
  8. Add drained noodles and sauce, toss quickly
  9. Stir fry for 3-4 minutes until noodles absorb sauce
  10. Add half the bean sprouts and green onions, toss
  11. Transfer to serving plate
  12. Top with remaining bean sprouts, peanuts, cilantro, lime, and chili flakes

Cooking Tips

Don't oversoak noodles or they become mushy. Cook over very high heat for authentic smoky flavor. Work quickly once noodles are added. Substitute chicken or tofu for shrimp. Add more sugar for sweeter pad thai. Squeeze fresh lime juice before eating for best taste.
