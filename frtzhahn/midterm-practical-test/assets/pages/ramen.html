Ramen

Japanese noodle soup with rich savory broth

Prep: 30 min Cook: 2 hours Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 4 packs fresh ramen noodles
  • 2 lbs pork bones
  • 1 lb pork belly, sliced
  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp miso paste
  • 4 soft boiled eggs
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 sheets nori seaweed
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Place pork bones in large pot with water and boil for 10 minutes, drain and rinse
  2. Add bones back to pot with chicken broth, garlic, and ginger
  3. Simmer on low heat for 2 hours to make rich broth
  4. Strain broth and discard solids
  5. Add soy sauce, miso paste, and sesame oil to broth
  6. Pan fry pork belly slices until golden brown
  7. Boil eggs for 6 minutes, then place in ice water and peel
  8. Cook ramen noodles according to package directions
  9. Divide noodles into bowls and pour hot broth over
  10. Top with pork belly, egg, bean sprouts, nori, and green onions
  11. Serve immediately while hot

Cooking Tips

For authentic tonkotsu broth, simmer bones for 8-12 hours for maximum flavor. Marinate soft boiled eggs in soy sauce overnight for ramen eggs. Add corn, butter, or chili oil for extra toppings. Don't overcook noodles, they should be firm.
