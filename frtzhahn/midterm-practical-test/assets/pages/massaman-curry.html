Massaman Curry

Rich and mild Thai curry with peanuts and potatoes

Prep: 20 min Cook: 45 min Servings: 4-6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs or beef chuck, cubed
  • 3 tbsp massaman curry paste
  • 2 cans (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 3 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 large onion, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat
  2. Add curry paste and fry for 2 minutes until fragrant
  3. Add thick coconut cream from top of can, stir for 3 minutes
  4. Add chicken or beef, cook until no longer pink
  5. Pour in remaining coconut milk and water
  6. Add potatoes, onion, bay leaves, cinnamon, and cardamom
  7. Bring to boil then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes
  8. Add fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind paste
  9. Stir in roasted peanuts
  10. Simmer for 10 more minutes until potatoes are tender
  11. Adjust seasoning with more fish sauce or sugar if needed
  12. Garnish with cilantro and serve with jasmine rice

Cooking Tips

Use thick coconut cream for richer curry. Toast peanuts for better flavor. Add more curry paste for spicier version. Palm sugar gives authentic sweetness. Let curry rest for 30 minutes before serving for flavors to develop. Tastes even better the next day.
